SIES

South Indian Education Society

Established in 1932 by Shri. M. V. Venkateshwaran, SIES is one of the oldest educational societies in India and is known for its rich tradition of imparting education while inculcating the essence of culture in its pupils. The society, since then has successfully expanded its reach by establishing various institutions that have striven to achieve academic excellence. The society has established a High school, an array of arts, science and commerce colleges, along with academic and professional institutions of higher learning. This includes establishments of Center for Excellence in Management Research and Development (CEMRD), SIES School of Packaging, SIES Indian Institute of Environment Management, and SIES Graduate School of Technology (an engineering institute). SIES Seniors Home and SIES Sri Chandrasekarendra Saraswati Veda Vidya Pitha for young children are the latest ventures initiated by SIES. Education blends with tradition at SIES, which in turn reflects on individual and personal growth. This has been achieved through the foresight, wisdom and dedication of the founders with a strong emphasis on high standards of academic, professional and societal performance. The South Indian Education Society (SIES), Which Has Been At The Forefront In The Field Of Education Since 1932, Imparting Quality Education Through Various Disciplines, Has Adopted The Rising Sun As Its Icon And Pledges Its Resolve To Take The Nation Further Up With Its Motto, 'RISE WITH EDUCATION'.

A NEW

RAY OF HOPE

SEAT was inauguarated by Bharat Ratna Dr. A.P.j. Abdul Kalam on august 15, 2008. Eduaction is the need of an hour. For the first time since our independence, the link between education and socio-economic development in our country has been realised. However, education cannot just be limited to certain pockets of society. The contemporary challenge is to make available quality higher education to all sections of our society.

The phenomenol cost of higher education in recent times have virtually shut educational gates to lower middle-class and poor sections of the society. This would lead to a large populate of youth not being not able to afford and thus enjoy the benefits of the of a decent collegiate education.

The SIES Education Assistance Trust (seat) is a small but benevolhigher ent step to address this issue and benefit the socity in its entirety. The trust aims to have a corplus fund of miniimum Rs 7.5 crore, representing 75 years of the Society's efforts in democratization of education. We expect that about 500 students would benefit from SEAT every year. In just a decade, we could have a substanicial number of students, reaping and benefits of the seat programme.The number could equal the student population of some of our college.
